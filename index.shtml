Christianity Against Religion

What if Christianity was originally the rejection of organized religion, and the embrace of life, God, and others outside the bounds of ritual and hierarchy?

Christianity at its core is non-ecclesiastical, non-institutional, and non-ritualistic. It is a fundamentally different approach to relationship with God, outside the bounds of religion as it was practiced in the ancient world, and as it continues to be practiced now.

Ideas here are drawn from the New Testament, and build upon the insights of the Restoration Movement, the Anabaptists, and the broad stream of historic Christian orthodoxy.
